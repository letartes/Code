📖 Liability Legend & References
CodeLiabilityRiskDescription
Deam-HDeamidation – High RiskHIGHNG and NS motifs. Asn–Gly is the most susceptible sequence to deamidation, forming succinimide intermediates rapidly at physiological pH. Asn–Ser is second most prone.
Deam-MDeamidation – Medium RiskMEDIUMNA, NT, NQ, NR, NH motifs and Gln-containing NG/NS equivalents (QG, QS). Glutamine can also deamidate, though more slowly than asparagine.
Ox-HOxidation – High RiskHIGHMet (M) and Trp (W). Methionine sulfoxide formation is a primary oxidative liability in mAbs; tryptophan oxidation (hydroxytryptophan, kynurenine) commonly observed under photo-oxidative stress.
Ox-MOxidation – Medium RiskMEDIUMHis (H) and Cys (C). Histidine is oxidised to 2-oxo-histidine under metal-catalysed oxidation conditions. Free cysteines are prone to disulfide scrambling and oxidation to sulfinic/sulfonic acid.
Ox-LOxidation – Low RiskLOWPhe (F) and Tyr (Y). These can oxidise under extreme photo- or metal-catalysed conditions but are generally lower concern.
Isom-HIsomerization – High RiskHIGHDG and DS motifs. Asp–Gly undergoes succinimide-mediated isomerization to isoAsp most readily; Asp–Ser is also highly prone. These result in backbone insertion of one CH₂ and altered charge.
Isom-MIsomerization – Medium RiskMEDIUMDT, DA, DN, DD, DH motifs. These aspartate-containing sequences can undergo slower isomerization relative to DG/DS.
N-GlycanN-Glycosylation Sequon (NXS/T, X≠P)INFOCanonical N-linked glycosylation sequon N–X–S/T where X is any amino acid except Pro. May represent intended or adventitious glycosylation. Impacts efficacy, PK, and immunogenicity.
O-GlycanO-Glycosylation Site (S/T-P motif)INFOSer–Pro and Thr–Pro motifs are characteristic O-glycosylation sites (mucin-type). Also flags isolated Ser/Thr clusters that may be O-glycosylated depending on cellular context.
Trunc-HTruncation – High RiskHIGHAsp–Pro (DP) bonds are acid-labile and cleave readily under mildly acidic purification/formulation conditions. N-terminal Gln (Q) and Glu (E) undergo spontaneous cyclisation to pyroglutamate, causing loss of one or two mass units and charge heterogeneity.
Trunc-MTruncation – Medium Risk (C-term clipping)MEDIUMC-terminal lysine clipping is a common post-translational modification in CHO-expressed mAbs driven by carboxypeptidase activity. N-terminal Met is subject to Met aminopeptidase removal and oxidation.
